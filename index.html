Systems I've Shaped

LiDAR & Camera-based Lane-Keeping in Unstructured Roads
Skandhan K  ·  Guided by Dr. Anuj Tiwari

What Autonomous vehicles struggle on roads of IITM, where animals make the scene unreliable and lane markings are unclear. Existing benchmarks were compute-heavy and not the best for real-time decision making for a on-campus shuttle.

How Designed a classical ML pipeline combining information from LiDAR point clouds with monocular camera frames. Built the perception stack from scratch: ground-plane segmentation, obstacle clustering, and a rule-based lateral controller, while deliberately keeping the stack simple, yet efficient for future edge deployment.

Result Delivered a reproducible, low-compute benchmark that future deep-learning approaches in the lab can be compared against; documented the full workflow as a starting point for autonomous driving research.

Delay Compensation in Networked Robotic Systems
Skandhan K, Shivendra Verma  ·  Guided by Dr. Anuj Tiwari

What Variable network latency causes instability and poor tracking in graphed robot networks , a long-standing, open problem in decentralized control of networked robots.

How Formulated a state-observer that estimates the delayed system state in real time, then feeds a predictive feedback signal to the controller. Modelled network delay as a bounded perturbation and validated on a simulated ground robot.

Result Observer-based compensation reduced cohesion loss (formation error while in transition) under 200 ms simulated delay significantly compared to an uncompensated baseline, demonstrating robustness across variable delay profiles.

Photoelastic Grain Solver (PeGS) for Larger Particles
Skandhan K, Shins K  ·  Guided by Prof. Ramesh K  ·  Daniels Lab, NC State

What Existing PeGS implementations was computationally restrictive for larger granular particles, where the fringe patterns grow complex and the existing methods proved to stall.

How Derived closed-form stress-field equations for soft photoelastic disks that replace existing method to formulate. Validated a scaling trick to match the experimental solution and integrated it into Mr. Shins PhD thesis.

Result Achieved a significant reduction in solve time for large-particle configurations, enabling the lab to run larger ensemble experiments. Put my skills and findings from the Young Investigator Workshop, to work ;).
